GATTE KI SUBJI RECIPE


ABOUT GATTE KI SABJI

Gatte Ki Sabji is a quintessential Rajasthani delicacy that brings the rich, aromatic flavors of India's desert state to your table. This traditional dish is a culinary masterpiece made from gram flour (besan) dumplings, known as "gatte," simmered in a tangy and spicy yogurt-based gravy. Originating from Rajasthan, a region known for its arid climate and scarce fresh vegetables, Gatte Ki Sabji is a perfect example of how simple ingredients can be transformed into a delectable feast. The gram flour dumplings, spiced with carom seeds and other aromatic spices, provide a delightful contrast to the creamy and slightly tangy yogurt gravy. This dish is a staple in Rajasthani households and is cherished for its unique texture and robust flavors. Gatte Ki Sabji is not only delicious but also nutritious, offering a good source of protein and fiber from the gram flour.

INGREDIENTS

For Gatte (Gram Flour Dumplings):

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon hing (asafoetida)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)



For Gravy:

  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon hing (asafoetida
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 large tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (whisked)
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

COOKING PROCESS

Preparing Gatte:

1.Mixing Dough:

  • In a mixing bowl, combine besan, yogurt, red chili powder, turmeric powder, ajwain, cumin seeds, hing, oil, and salt.
  • Gradually add water and knead into a firm dough. Divide into 3-4 equal portions.
    •

    2.Shaping and Boiling:

    • Roll each portion into a long, thin cylinder.
    • Bring water to a boil in a large pan. Add the rolled dough cylinders and boil for 10-12 minutes until they float to the surface and are cooked through.
    • Remove from water and let them cool slightly. Cut into 1/2-inch pieces (gatte).

    Preparing Gravy:

    1.Base of the Gravy:

    • Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add hing and sauté briefly.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté until fragrant.

    2.Tomato and Spice Mixture:

    • Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala
    • Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.

    3.Combining Gatte and Gravy:

    • Lower the heat and add whisked yogurt, stirring continuously to avoid curdling.
    • Add the boiled gatte pieces to the gravy. Stir gently to coat them well.
    • Add water to adjust the consistency of the gravy. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

    4.Finishing Touches:

    • Sprinkle garam masala and mix gently. Simmer for another 2 minutes..
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.


    Special Safety Measures

    1.Handling Hot Ingredients:

    • Always be cautious when working with boiling water and hot oil. Use heat-resistant gloves if necessary.

    2.Preventing Yogurt Curdling:

    • Ensure the heat is low when adding yogurt to prevent curdling. Stir continuously while adding.

    3.Spice Handling:

    • Be careful when handling spices, especially red chili powder. Avoid direct contact with your eyes.

    Serving Suggestions

    • Serve Gatte Ki Sabji hot with steamed rice or Indian bread like roti, paratha, or naan.
    • Pair it with a side of raita or a simple salad for a complete meal.

    Additional Tips

    • For an extra flavor boost, you can add a pinch of kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) towards the end of cooking.
    • Adjust the spice levels according to your preference. The recipe can be made milder or spicier as needed.

    Enjoy the rich and authentic taste of Rajasthan with this delightful Gatte Ki Sabji!
